## LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS

### EU Court Case C-84/24: Impact on Decision

**Case Status**: Pending before EU Court of Justice (filed February 2024)

**Legal Question**: Whether funds and economic resources of a company should be frozen when a designated person holds exactly 50% of shares

**Advocate General Opinion** (July 3, 2025):
- Asset freeze on designated person does not **automatically** extend to non-listed company
- However, 50% ownership creates **rebuttable presumption** of control
- Banks may **provisionally freeze** assets while conducting further due diligence
- Company must demonstrate designated person does NOT control funds/economic resources

**Impact on KYC Decision**:
- **NO IMPACT** - This case concerns EU sanctions application within EU jurisdiction
- U.S. financial institutions must comply with **U.S. OFAC regulations** independently
- OFAC's 50% rule: U.S. persons prohibited from dealing with entities 50%+ owned by SDNs
- Shakutin's 50% ownership meets OFAC threshold for prohibition
- **Additionally**: Entity is subject to Lithuanian national sanctions (separate from ownership question)
- **Additionally**: Shakutin is also on OFAC SDN list, UK sanctions, and Swiss sanctions

**Conclusion**: Even if EU court rules in company's favor, U.S. bank obligations under OFAC remain unchanged.

## DECISION RATIONALE

### Why This Application Must Be Rejected

**Primary Reason**: OFAC Sanctions Compliance

Under OFAC's 50 Percent Rule:
> "U.S. persons are generally prohibited from dealing with individuals or entities that are 50 percent or more owned, directly or indirectly, by one or more blocked persons."

- Aleksandr Shakutin is a blocked person (OFAC SDN List, BELARUS-EO14038)
- UAB "EM SYSTEM" is 50% owned by Aleksandr Shakutin
- Therefore, UAB "EM SYSTEM" is a blocked entity under the 50 Percent Rule
- U.S. financial institutions cannot open accounts or conduct transactions with blocked entities

**Secondary Reasons**:

1. **Multiple Sanctions Jurisdictions**: Entity's beneficial owner is sanctioned by EU, US, UK, Switzerland - indicating global consensus on prohibition

2. **Active Asset Freeze**: Lithuanian banks have already frozen the company's accounts (‚Ç¨14 million), demonstrating that the entity is subject to sanctions enforcement in its home jurisdiction

3. **Sanctions Evasion Concerns**: Credible investigative journalism reports that this entity is being used to evade sanctions and conduct prohibited business activities in Germany

4. **Reputational Risk**: Association with "Lukashenko's wallet" and support for repressive authoritarian regime creates unacceptable reputational risk for the bank

5. **Regulatory Risk**: Onboarding this customer would expose the bank to:
   - OFAC civil penalties (up to $330,120 per violation or 2x transaction value)
   - OFAC criminal penalties (up to $1,000,000 per violation)
   - Regulatory enforcement actions
   - Consent orders and compliance monitoring
   - License revocation risk

### Risk-Based Approach

FATF risk-based approach requires enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers. However, when a customer presents PROHIBITED risk due to sanctions, **no amount of due diligence can mitigate the risk**. The only compliant action is rejection.
